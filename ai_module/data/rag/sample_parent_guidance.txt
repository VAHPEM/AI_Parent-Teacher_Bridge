Parent communication guidelines (sample knowledge for RAG)

When discussing academic progress with families, use clear, non-judgmental language. Focus on specific behaviors and work samples rather than labels.

For students who are struggling, suggest small, consistent routines: short daily reading or practice, a quiet homework space, and checking in with the teacher about one concrete skill at a time.

For students who are doing well, reinforce effort and habits—not only grades—and invite parents to ask open-ended questions about what the child found interesting or challenging this week.

If scores fluctuate, explain that variability is normal and that trends over several weeks matter more than a single week. Encourage parents to share context (illness, schedule changes) so teachers can interpret patterns fairly.
